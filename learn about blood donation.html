Who can donate?

You need to be 18-65 years old, weight 45kg or more and be fit and healthy.

The main types of blood donation:

Whole Blood Donation:

Donors give a pint (about 470 ml) of whole blood, which contains red blood cells, plasma, and platelets.

Red Cell Donation:

In this type of donation, a machine separates the red blood cells from the other components, returning the plasma and platelets to the donor.

Platelet Donation:

Platelets are a component of blood that helps in clotting. Some patients, especially those undergoing chemotherapy, benefit from platelet donations.

Plasma Donation:

Plasma is the liquid component of blood that carries red and white blood cells and platelets. Plasma donation is important for patients with certain medical conditions.
